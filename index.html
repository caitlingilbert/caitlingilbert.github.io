Caitlin Gilbert

Data Journalist  ·  Neuroscientist

LinkedIn ↗ GitHub ↗ Resume ↗

I'm an award-winning data reporter based in New York who uses code to report health, science, and tech stories at The Washington Post . I've led investigations into dietitan influencers, subminimum wages paid to disabled workers, RFK Jr.'s anti-vaccine rhetoric, compulsive use of TikTok, and declining vaccination rates in the US. My work has won a SABEW award and NCDJ's Katherine Schneider disability reporting award. I have been a finalist for Goldsmith and NIHCM awards for investigative reporting in 2025 and 2026. Previously I worked at the Financial Times & Reuters. For my neuroscience PhD work on songbird brains at Rockefeller University, I studied genomics, language development, neuroethology, developmental neurobiology and evolution.
